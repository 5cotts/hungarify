]# Hungarian Locative Cases: -ban/-ben vs -n/-on/-en/-ön

## Overview

Hungarian has two main locative cases for expressing location:
- **-ban/-ben** = Inessive case ("in" / "inside")
- **-n/-on/-en/-ön** = Superessive case ("on" / "at")

## -ban/-ben (Inessive Case = "in")

### Meaning
Expresses being **inside** something or **within** boundaries.

### Vowel Harmony Rules
- **-ban** = for **low vowel words** (a, á, o, ó, u, ú)
- **-ben** = for **high/mixed vowel words** (i, í, é, e, ö, ő, ü, ű)

### Examples

| Noun | Type | Inessive Form | Meaning |
|------|------|---------------|---------|
| ház (house) | Low | ház**ban** | in the house |
| autó (car) | Low | autó**ban** | in the car |
| doboz (box) | Low | doboz**ban** | in the box |
| kert (garden) | Mixed | kert**ben** | in the garden |
| szék (chair) | High | szék**ben** | in the chair |
| város (city) | Low | város**ban** | in the city |

### Usage Examples
- **"A könyv a házban van"** = The book is in the house
- **"A macska a dobozban van"** = The cat is in the box
- **"Autóban ülök"** = I'm sitting in the car

---

## -n/-on/-en/-ön (Superessive Case = "on" / "at")

### Meaning
Expresses being **on top of** something or **at** a location.

### Vowel Harmony Rules
- **-n/-on** = for **low vowel words** (a, á, o, ó, u, ú)
- **-en/-ön** = for **high/mixed vowel words** (i, í, é, e, ö, ő, ü, ű)

### Examples

| Noun | Type | Superessive Form | Meaning |
|------|------|------------------|---------|
| asztal (table) | Low | asztal**on** | on the table |
| pad (bench) | Low | pad**on** | on the bench |
| ház (house) | Low | ház**on** | on the house |
| szék (chair) | High | szék**en** | on the chair |
| kert (garden) | Mixed | kert**en** | on the garden |

### Usage Examples
- **"A könyv az asztalon van"** = The book is on the table
- **"A macska a padon van"** = The cat is on the bench
- **"A madár a házon van"** = The bird is on the house

---

## When to Use Which?

### Use -ban/-ben when:
- Something is **inside** or **within** boundaries
- Referring to containers, buildings, enclosed spaces
- Examples:
  - in a room → **szobában**
  - in a box → **dobozban**
  - in a car → **autóban**
  - in a house → **házban**

### Use -n/-on/-en/-ön when:
- Something is **on top of** something
- Referring to surfaces
- **Many city names** (even though it means "in")
- Examples:
  - on a table → **asztalon**
  - on a chair → **széken**
  - in Budapest → **Budapesten**
  - in New York → **New Yorkban** (some cities use -ban)

---

## Special Cases: Cities and Places

Many city names use the **superessive case** (-n/-on/-en/-ön) even though it translates as "in":

| City | Form | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| Budapest | **Budapesten** | in Budapest |
| Pécs | **Pécsen** | in Pécs |
| Debrecen | **Debrecenben** | in Debrecen |
| Szeged | **Szegeden** | in Szeged |

**Note:** Some cities use **-ban/-ben** instead:
- **Párizsban** = in Paris
- **Londonban** = in London

There's no strict rule - you need to learn which case each city uses.

## Key Points

1. **-ban/-ben** = inside, within boundaries
2. **-n/-on/-en/-ön** = on top of, at a location
3. **Vowel harmony** determines which variant to use:
   - Low vowels (a, á, o, ó, u, ú) → -ban, -n/-on
   - High/mixed vowels (i, í, é, e, ö, ő, ü, ű) → -ben, -en/-ön
4. **City names** often use superessive case (-n/-on/-en/-ön) even for "in"
5. Some city names must be memorized individually
