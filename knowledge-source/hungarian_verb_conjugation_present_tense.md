# Hungarian Verb Conjugation - Present Tense

## Overview

Hungarian verbs have two conjugation types:
- **Indefinite conjugation** - when the object is indefinite or there's no object
- **Definite conjugation** - when the object is definite (has definite article or is specific)

---

## Indefinite Conjugation

Used when:
- The object is indefinite (no article or "egy" = a/an)
- There is no object
- The verb is intransitive

### Personal Endings

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **1st (I/we)** | -ok/-ek/-ök | -unk/-ünk |
| **2nd (you)** | -sz/-ol/-el/-öl | -tok/-tek/-tök |
| **3rd (he/she/it/they)** | -∅ (no ending) | -nak/-nek |

### Vowel Harmony Rules

**1st Person Singular (-ok/-ek/-ök):**
- **-ok** = after low vowel words (a, á, o, ó, u, ú)
- **-ek/-ök** = after high vowel words (i, í, é, e, ö, ő, ü, ű)
  - **-ek** = after unrounded high vowels (i, í, é, e)
  - **-ök** = after rounded high vowels (ö, ő)

**2nd Person Singular (-sz/-ol/-el/-öl):**
- **-sz** = after most verbs (most common)
- **-ol/-el/-öl** = after certain verb stems ending in consonants
  - **-ol** = after low vowel words
  - **-el** = after high vowel words (unrounded)
  - **-öl** = after high vowel words (rounded)

**3rd Person Singular:**
- No ending (just the verb stem)

**1st Person Plural (-unk/-ünk):**
- **-unk** = after low vowel words
- **-ünk** = after high vowel words

**2nd Person Plural (-tok/-tek/-tök):**
- **-tok** = after low vowel words
- **-tek/-tök** = after high vowel words
  - **-tek** = after unrounded high vowels
  - **-tök** = after rounded high vowels

**3rd Person Plural (-nak/-nek):**
- **-nak** = after low vowel words
- **-nek** = after high vowel words

---

## Definite Conjugation

Used when:
- The object is definite (has definite article "a/az" or "the")
- The object is a proper noun
- The object is a possessive form (my, your, etc.)

### Personal Endings

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **1st (I/we)** | -om/-em/-öm | -juk/-jük |
| **2nd (you)** | -od/-ed/-öd | -játok/-itek/-itek |
| **3rd (he/she/it/they)** | -ja/-je/-i | -ják/-jék/-ik |

### Vowel Harmony Rules

**1st Person Singular (-om/-em/-öm):**
- **-om** = after low vowel words
- **-em/-öm** = after high vowel words
  - **-em** = after unrounded high vowels
  - **-öm** = after rounded high vowels

**2nd Person Singular (-od/-ed/-öd):**
- **-od** = after low vowel words
- **-ed/-öd** = after high vowel words
  - **-ed** = after unrounded high vowels
  - **-öd** = after rounded high vowels

**3rd Person Singular (-ja/-je/-i):**
- **-ja** = after low vowel words
- **-je/-i** = after high vowel words
  - **-je** = after unrounded high vowels
  - **-i** = after certain high vowel verbs

**1st Person Plural (-juk/-jük):**
- **-juk** = after low vowel words
- **-jük** = after high vowel words

**2nd Person Plural (-játok/-itek/-itek):**
- **-játok** = after low vowel words
- **-itek/-itek** = after high vowel words

**3rd Person Plural (-ják/-jék/-ik):**
- **-ják** = after low vowel words
- **-jék/-ik** = after high vowel words

## Common Verb Examples

### "lenni" (to be)

| Person | Indefinite | Definite |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **1st singular** | vagyok | vagyok |
| **2nd singular** | vagy | vagy |
| **3rd singular** | van | van |
| **1st plural** | vagyunk | vagyunk |
| **2nd plural** | vagytok | vagytok |
| **3rd plural** | vannak | vannak |

**Note:** "lenni" doesn't have a definite/indefinite distinction in present tense.

### "látni" (to see)

**Indefinite Conjugation:**
- látok (I see something)
- látsz (you see something)
- lát (he/she sees something)
- látunk (we see something)
- láttok (you all see something)
- látnak (they see something)

**Definite Conjugation:**
- látom (I see it)
- látod (you see it)
- látja (he/she sees it)
- látjuk (we see it)
- látjátok (you all see it)
- látják (they see it)

**Examples:**
- **"Látok egy házat"** = I see a house (indefinite)
- **"Látom a házat"** = I see the house (definite)

## Verb Types and Patterns

### Type 1: Regular -ni verbs

Most verbs end in **-ni** in the infinitive and follow regular patterns:
- **tanulni** (to learn)
- **beszélni** (to speak)
- **szeretni** (to love)

### Type 2: -ik verbs

Some verbs end in **-ik** in 3rd person singular:
- **eszik** (he/she eats)
- **iszik** (he/she drinks)
- **alszik** (he/she sleeps)

### Type 3: Irregular verbs

Some verbs have irregular forms:
- **lenni** (to be) - vagyok, vagy, van, etc.
- **menni** (to go) - megyek, mész, megy, etc.
- **jönni** (to come) - jövök, jössz, jön, etc.

---

## Key Rules Summary

1. **Vowel harmony** determines which ending variant to use (-ok vs -ek, -unk vs -ünk, etc.)

2. **Definite vs Indefinite:**
   - Use **indefinite** when object is indefinite or there's no object
   - Use **definite** when object is definite (has "a/az" or is specific)

3. **1st person singular** endings depend on vowel harmony:
   - Low vowels → -ok, -om
   - High vowels → -ek/-ök, -em/-öm

4. **2nd person singular** usually uses -sz, but some verbs use -ol/-el/-öl

5. **3rd person singular** indefinite has no ending, definite uses -ja/-je/-i

6. **Plural forms** follow vowel harmony strictly

---

## Practice Tips

1. Learn the vowel harmony rules first
2. Practice with common verbs (látni, tanulni, beszélni)
3. Pay attention to definite vs indefinite object
4. Memorize irregular verbs (lenni, menni, jönni)
5. Practice with real sentences, not just conjugation tables

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mixing definite/indefinite:** Don't use definite conjugation with indefinite objects
2. **Vowel harmony errors:** Make sure endings match the verb's vowel type
3. **Forgetting 3rd person:** 3rd person singular indefinite has NO ending
4. **Irregular verbs:** Remember that "lenni", "menni", "jönni" are irregular
